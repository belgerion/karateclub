Requirements By Belt

Each belt after the white belt has to be earned. To earn them, you know and be able to demonstrate from memory, the various stances, strikes, blocks, kicks and kata. Below is a break down of each of the individual belt requirements.

In addition to the requirements listed below, each belt has a list of questions and answers that must be answered. These questions/answers are listed as a separate section of this website.

Self Defense
    Escapes from a Single Wrist Grab
    • twist wrist to align it with the opponents thumb
    • quickly and firmly pull your wrist toward the opposite shoulder
    • assisted elbow strike

    • tuck elbow of grabbed hand into your side
    • quickly shoot your grabbed hand behind the opponents head and grab the back of their head
    • grab the opponents chin with free hand
    • with the free hand, push the chin away as the grabbed hand pulls the back of the head forward

    • free hand knife hand strike to the opponents shoulder
    • raise grabbed hand to shoulder height
    • stomp on the opponents foot at the same time execute a knife hand strike to the ribs with grabbed hand

    • fake swinging grabbed hand to the outside, then reverse it to the inside
    • with free hand, grab the opponents hand and twist into a wrist lock
    • step in and perform a rising elbow strike to the head, then back down into the collarbone

    • fake swinging grabbed hand outside, then reverse and perform a wrist bar
    • step in and perform a dropping elbow strike to break the elbow
    • step inside and spin under the opponents arm and twist the arm, putting the opponent in a high shoulder lock

Self Defense
    Escapes from a Cross Wrist Grab (Opponent's right hand grabs your right wrist)
    • with the right hand, grasp the back of the opponent's head
    • execute a left roundhouse elbow strike to the opponent's head

    • rotate your right hand clockwise and grab the opponent's hand with the fingers against the palm and the thumb on the back of the opponent's hand
    • once free, execute an upward elbow strike to the head
    • execute a dropping elbow strike to the collar bone

    • rotate the grabbed wrist around and over the opponents hand, applying pressure to the opponent's wrist with the knife edge
    • once free, execute a roundhouse elbow strike

    • step in with the left left and pivot to the right
    • bring the left arm up uder the opponent's right elbow, while pulling down on the opponent's right arm
    • after breaking the elbow, grasp the opponent's wrist with your left hand
    • reverse the motion, lifting the opponent's right arm, and ducking under while rotating the opponent's wrist
    • place your right hand on the opponent's right elbow and apply forward pressure on the elbow while pulling back on the hand

    • step in with the left left and pivot to the right
    • bring the left arm up uder the opponent's right elbow, while pulling down on the opponent's right arm
    • after breaking the elbow, grasp the opponent's wrist with both your hands
    • lifting the opponent's right arm, and ducking under while rotating the opponent's arm to face the palm toward the shoulder
    • pull the opponent's hand back and down

Self Defense
    Escapes from a Double Wrist Grab (opponent grabs both of your wrists)
    • step forward to bring elbows close to your side
    • quickly thrust your arms up and grasp the opponent's head
    • pull the head down for a knee strike

    • step forward and pivot while straightening the arm now closest to the opponent
    • once your arm is free, execute a supported elbow strike to the opponent's head

    • step forward while pulling your arms back toward your belt
    • once your opponent's arms are fully extend, reverse the motion, pressing up and back on the opponent's wrists with your hands
    • execute a front push kick to the opponent's stomach

    • step back, at the same time rotating your wrists to bring the knife edge to the top of the opponent's wrists
    • push down sharply on the opponent's wrists
    • execute a knee strike when the opponent bends over

    • turn your right wrist upward
    • grasp the opponent's left hand with your left hand, your fingers on the palm, thumb on the back of their hand
    • firmly pull your right hand free, while applying pressure to opponent's left wrist

    Defense against a Knife Attack
    1. Defense against a downward stab
    • perform a left rising block to the opponent's wrist, leaving the block in place
    • execute a right palm heel strike to the opponent's chin
    • reach under the opponent's arm with your right hand and grab your left hand
    • pull the opponent's arm backwards and down, driving them to the floor

    2. Defense against a straight stab to the midsection
    • step off center behind the hand to dodge the strike, while performing a left palm block
    • grab the opponent's with both of your hands, and twist the opponent's wrist
    • step forward with right foot and drive the opponent to the ground
    • twist the opponent's wrist until they drop the knife

    3. Defense against an Upward Thrust
    • perform a low section X block near the opponent's elbow
    • twist the wrists over to secure the opponent's arm
    • twist to the right, pulling the opponent's arm past your body
    • execute a left thrust kick to the opponent's abdomen
    • twist the opponent's hand up and over their shoulder, driving them to the ground

    4. Defense against a Downward Slash (left to right)
    • perform a left rising block to the opponent's wrist and a right knife hand strike to the opponent's temple
    • grab the opponent's wrist with both hands, bringing it down in front of your body as you twist
    • break the elbow over your shoulder
    • twist the opponent's hand toward their body, with your right hand on their wrist and your left hand on their elbow
    • thrust the opponent's knife into their abdomen

    5. Defense against a Back Slash (right to left)
    • perform a window block to the right
    • grab the opponent's wrist with your right hand, while executing a left elbow strike to the ribs
    • execute an arc hand strike to the opponent's elbow, driving the knife into the opponent's abdomen
    Escapes from a Hair Grab
    1. Escape from a One Hand Grab to the Front of the Head
    • grab the opponent's hand and hold them to your head
    • bend at the waist and head, while stepping back, and pulling down on the opponent's wrist
    • once released, push the opponent's hand away and execute a front snap kick to the opponent's midsection

    2. Escape from a Two Hand Grab to the Sides of the Head
    • grab the opponent's hands with both your hands
    • bring your elbow up and over the opponent's arms, locking them under your armpit

    3. Escape from a Two Hand Grab to the Side and Front of the Head
    • grab the opponent's hands with both your hands
    • bring your elbow up and over the opponent's arms, locking them under your armpit

    4. Escape from a One Hand Grab to the Rear of the Head
    • grab opponent's hand with both of your hands
    • turn towards the opponent's pinky, twisting the hand off the head, and turning the palm towards you
    • apply pressure to the opponent's wrist by pushing against the palm

    5. Escape from a Two Hand Grab to the Sides of the Head from the Rear
    • grab both the opponent's hands with yours
    • twist under the opponent's arms in either direction
    • once facing the opponent, straighten your arms forcing the opponent's into a pretzel
